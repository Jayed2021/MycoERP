# Reports and Analytics

The Reports page provides production analytics and trend data to help managers understand farm performance, identify issues, and make decisions about operations.

## Who Can Access Reports

The Reports module is available to Farm Managers and Administrators. Other roles (lab workers, production workers, harvest workers) do not have access to this page unless their permissions are specifically modified.

## Accessing Reports

Navigate to "Reports" in the sidebar (under the Reporting group). The reports page offers multiple views of your farm's production data.

## Available Report Types

### Pipeline Overview

Shows a visual representation of how many batches are at each stage of the production pipeline:

- Culture (agar plates and liquid culture)
- Spawn (grain spawn)
- Substrate (prepared substrate)
- Fruiting (active fruiting blocks)
- Harvest (recently harvested)

This helps you see bottlenecks -- if substrate is piling up but fruiting is low, you may need more fruiting room capacity.

### Yield Trends

Displays harvest data over time:

- Total harvest weight per week or month.
- Breakdown by species.
- Grade A vs Grade B vs Waste proportions.
- Average yield per fruiting block.
- Trends over selected date ranges.

Use this to track whether production is increasing, stable, or declining, and whether quality (Grade A percentage) is improving.

### Contamination Trends

Shows contamination incidents over time:

- Number of reports filed per week or month.
- Breakdown by severity level.
- Breakdown by batch type (where in the pipeline contamination most often occurs).
- Most common suspected types.

A sudden increase in contamination reports may indicate a systemic issue that needs investigation (faulty autoclave, contaminated spawn source, procedural lapse).

### Task Metrics

Provides insight into operational efficiency:

- Task completion rate (what percentage of tasks are completed on time).
- Average time to complete tasks.
- Number of overdue tasks.
- Approval rates (what percentage are approved vs rejected on first submission).
- Breakdown by task type or assigned role.

This helps managers identify where workflows are slow or where additional training may be needed.

## Using Date Range Filters

All reports support date range filtering:

1. Select a start date and end date at the top of the reports page.
2. The visualizations and data update to show only the selected period.
3. Common ranges: last 7 days, last 30 days, last 90 days, year to date.

Comparing different time periods helps identify seasonal patterns and measure the impact of operational changes.

## Interpreting the Data

When reviewing reports:

- **Look for trends, not individual data points.** A single bad week does not indicate a problem; a consistent downward trend does.
- **Compare similar periods.** Production may naturally vary by season, so compare this month to the same month last year if possible.
- **Investigate anomalies.** A sudden spike in contamination or drop in yield warrants immediate investigation.
- **Track the impact of changes.** After implementing a new SOP or changing a process, watch the metrics over the following weeks to see if they improve.

## Best Practices

- Review reports weekly during team meetings to keep everyone informed about farm performance.
- Use yield data to calculate biological efficiency (fresh weight / dry substrate weight) per species and strain.
- Track contamination rates as a key performance indicator. A well-run farm should aim for less than 5% contamination rate.
- Use task metrics to balance workload across team members and roles.
- Export or screenshot key metrics for monthly performance reviews and business planning.
