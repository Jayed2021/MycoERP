# Harvest Recording

When fruiting blocks produce mushrooms, the harvest must be recorded to track yields, calculate production efficiency, and maintain traceability.

## When to Record a Harvest

Record a harvest each time you pick mushrooms from a fruiting batch. Most batches produce multiple flushes (rounds of fruiting), so you will typically record several harvests per batch over its productive life.

## Creating a Harvest Record

1. Navigate to "Harvests" in the sidebar.
2. Click "New Harvest".
3. Fill in the required fields:
   - **Fruiting Batch** -- Select the fruiting block batch the mushrooms came from.
   - **Species** -- Auto-fills from the selected batch but can be confirmed.
   - **Strain** -- Auto-fills from the selected batch.
   - **Flush Number** -- Which flush this is (1 for the first harvest, 2 for the second, etc.).
   - **Harvested At** -- Date and time of harvest (defaults to now).
   - **Gross Weight** -- Total weight of all harvested mushrooms in grams or kilograms.
4. Enter the weight breakdown:
   - **Grade A Weight** -- Premium quality mushrooms suitable for fresh market sale.
   - **Grade B Weight** -- Acceptable quality mushrooms (slight cosmetic issues but edible).
   - **Waste Weight** -- Mushrooms that cannot be sold (damaged, overripe, too small).
5. Optional fields:
   - **Notes** -- Any observations about the harvest (size, color, density, etc.).
   - **Photo** -- A photo of the harvest for quality records.
6. Click "Save Harvest".

A unique harvest code is generated automatically (e.g., HV-0001).

## Weight Breakdown

The total gross weight should equal Grade A + Grade B + Waste:

- **Grade A** -- Perfect specimens: uniform size, no blemishes, correct maturity, suitable for fresh retail.
- **Grade B** -- Minor imperfections: slightly uneven, small tears, slightly over/under-mature, but perfectly edible. May go to restaurants, processing, or secondary markets.
- **Waste** -- Not sellable: heavily damaged, contaminated surface, wilted, or too small to justify processing.

The net usable weight (Grade A + Grade B) is what counts toward production metrics.

## Flush Numbers

Each time a fruiting block produces a new crop of mushrooms, that is a "flush":

- **Flush 1** -- The first harvest from a block. Usually the largest yield.
- **Flush 2** -- Second round of fruiting after the block re-hydrates. Often slightly smaller.
- **Flush 3** -- Third round. Diminishing returns.
- **Further flushes** -- Some species can produce 4 or more flushes.

Record each flush as a separate harvest record, incrementing the flush number.

## Harvest Approval

If approval is configured, a manager reviews the harvest record:

1. The manager checks the weights make sense (Grade A + Grade B + Waste = Gross).
2. The manager verifies the flush number is correct.
3. The manager checks the photo if provided.
4. If everything looks good, they mark it as approved.

## Viewing Harvest History

On the Harvests page:

- See all harvest records in reverse chronological order.
- Filter by species, strain, or batch.
- View total yields per batch across all flushes.
- Check which batches are nearing the end of their productive life.

From a batch detail page:

- Go to the "Harvest" tab to see all harvests from that specific batch.
- See the total cumulative yield across all flushes.

## Best Practices

- Weigh harvests immediately after picking, before mushrooms lose moisture.
- Be honest with grading -- over-grading as Grade A leads to customer complaints; under-grading loses revenue.
- Take photos of each flush to document mushroom quality and size over time.
- Record harvest time accurately -- this helps calculate harvest windows and predict future flushes.
- If a batch shows declining yields or poor quality, note this in the harvest record so managers can decide when to retire the batch.
- When the final flush is harvested and the block is spent, update the batch status to "Spent" or "Closed".
